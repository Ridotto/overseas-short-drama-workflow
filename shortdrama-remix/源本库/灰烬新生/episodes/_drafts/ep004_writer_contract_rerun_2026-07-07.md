# Episode 4: The Birthday They Forgot

> fresh first draft by narrowed writer input contract  
> purpose: `/episode`-only validation, no `/dialogue-polish`

## Scene 1

**INT. WHITMORE PARLOR - LATE AFTERNOON**

A seamstress lifts a pale blue dress against Celeste. Margaret steps back to admire the line of the shoulders. Nathan checks a handwritten guest list. Cole comes in with a cake box balanced on one arm.

The room is busy in the warm, careless way only happy families get to be.

Evelyn stands in the doorway with the empty brooch box still in her hand.

No one notices her first.

**MARGARET:** "Not that ribbon. The blue one softens her."

**COLE:** "I told the bakery white roses, not red."

**NATHAN:** "Six o'clock for dinner. Seven for the toasts."

Celeste laughs under her breath.

**CELESTE:** "You're all doing too much."

Victor looks at her and, for once, there is something almost gentle in his face.

**VICTOR:** "For your birthday? Not enough."

The word lands late.

Birthday.

Evelyn's fingers close around the box.

## Scene 2

**INT. WHITMORE HALLWAY - CONTINUOUS**

Evelyn steps back before anyone has to ask her to.

The hallway is quieter. She looks down at the brooch box in her hand, then at the flowers passing by in servants' arms.

White roses.

Blue ribbon.

One iced cake.

She says it to herself before she means to.

**EVELYN:** "It's mine too."

The sentence is so small it almost disappears.

For a flash, another birthday breaks through:

Grandmother Rose leaning over a tiny cake.

Nathan stealing frosting with his finger.

Cole swearing they'd never forget again.

Victor saying a Whitmore birthday was never missed.

Back in the hallway, the house moves around her as if she were furniture.

Wilson hurries up with Evelyn's coat draped over one arm.

An Ashford envelope slips from the pocket and falls open at their feet.

Wilson bends first.

Her eyes move once over the page.

Then once more.

**WILSON:** "Miss Evelyn..."

Evelyn reaches for it.

Wilson doesn't let go right away.

**WILSON:** "This transfer."

Her voice drops.

**WILSON:** "You saved them."

## Scene 3

**INT. WHITMORE HALLWAY - CONTINUOUS**

From the parlor, laughter spills out around Celeste's name.

Wilson turns toward it, already angry.

**WILSON:** "They pinned Miss Rose's brooch on the wrong girl, and now this—"

She takes one step.

Evelyn catches her sleeve.

Not hard.

Just enough.

**EVELYN:** "Please don't."

Wilson turns back, stunned.

**WILSON:** "They should know."

Evelyn shakes her head.

**EVELYN:** "No."

Wilson looks at the paper. At Evelyn. Back toward the parlor.

**WILSON:** "After what they've done to you?"

Evelyn takes the envelope from her this time and folds it closed.

**EVELYN:** "I leave tonight."

Another burst of laughter. Glasses clink. Someone calls for more candles.

Wilson's face breaks from anger into something worse.

Pity.

**WILSON:** "And your birthday?"

Evelyn presses the envelope flat against her coat.

Her eyes shine once, then go still.

**EVELYN:** "Let them have theirs."

The grandfather clock starts to strike.

Seven hours until the Ashford family comes for the bride.
